Why another tuner?

Because most of them are not good enough to perform with

Most tuners bundled into other apps share the same failure modes: they jitter when locking onto a pitch, lag by a quarter second, lose tracking in a room with other instruments, and require you to hold still and be patient. Those constraints are not acceptable on stage between songs or when you need to re-tune mid-rehearsal without stopping the room.

SongScribe Tuner was built because we needed something better for ourselves. We tested pitch detection against hardware clip-on tuners. We worked on the needle smoothing until it tracked accurately without jitter. We made the instrument presets correct: string names and expected frequencies are right for each instrument, not approximated from a guitar model. We did not ship it until we had used it at rehearsals and found nothing to complain about.

Under the hood

Built to track pitch accurately in a noisy room

Most tuner apps hand your microphone signal straight to the operating system and ask it to do the hard work. The OS applies its own noise suppression, echo cancellation, and automatic gain correction. Those tools are designed for phone calls, not for musical pitch detection, and the results show: jitter, latency, and false readings when other instruments are playing nearby.

SongScribe Tuner takes control of the full audio pipeline. OS noise processing is disabled entirely. Instead, the signal passes through a bandpass filter tuned to the musically relevant frequency range — low enough to catch the fundamental of an open bass string, high enough to track violin. Silence and ambient noise are gated out before pitch detection runs, so the algorithm is never working on a signal that has nothing in it. Once a candidate frequency is found, the harmonic structure of the sound is analysed to confirm the correct octave. A confidence score is computed over a short rolling window of readings, and the needle does not commit to a new position until that score crosses a threshold. The result is a needle that locks deliberately and holds steady rather than one that flickers at every breath of background noise.

None of this is magic. It is just a carefully assembled chain where each step does one job, and the next step only runs when the previous one passes its check. We tested it against hardware clip-on tuners until the readings matched.

Design philosophy

One thing. Done properly.

A tuner has one job. Everything in the UI is arranged around that job. The needle is large because you need to read it at arm's length while holding an instrument. The note name is large because that is the first thing you need to know. The cents display is smaller because it is secondary information. There are no feature tours, no rate prompts mid-session, no banners while you are actively tuning.

We did not add features to make the app feel bigger. We added the instrument presets because the string names and frequencies matter and generic guitar approximations are wrong for banjo. We added the themes because staring at a screen for two hours of rehearsal is better when the screen does not look clinical. We added the wallpaper option because some people have a specific aesthetic and one size does not fit all. Everything else we looked at and decided it did not belong.

Common questions

Does it work offline?
Yes. The tuner requires no internet connection. All pitch detection runs on the device. You can tune in airplane mode, in a basement, or anywhere else with no signal.
Does it record my audio?
No. The microphone is used for real-time pitch detection only. No audio is recorded, stored, or transmitted anywhere. Once you close the app, nothing from your microphone is retained. This is verifiable: the app requests only the microphone permission, and it uses it only while the tuner screen is active.
What instruments does it support?
22 presets across 6 instruments: guitar (8 tunings including DADGAD, Open G, and Drop D), bass (4-string and 5-string), ukulele (High G, Low G, Baritone, D Tuning), mandolin, banjo (Open G, Double C, Sawmill), and violin. Each preset shows the correct string names and expected pitches. Chromatic mode lets you tune any instrument to any note without selecting a preset first.
Is there a subscription?
No. The app is free to download and use with ads. Removing ads is a one-time $1.99 purchase. That is the only purchase available. There is no subscription, no monthly fee, and no in-app purchases beyond ad removal.
If I buy ad removal, does it carry over if I get a new phone?
Yes. The purchase is tied to your Apple ID through the App Store. Use the "Restore Purchases" option in the app on any new device signed into the same Apple ID, and ad removal will be restored at no additional cost.
When do ads appear?
Ads appear only on non-tuning screens: settings, instrument selection, and similar navigation screens. The main tuning display never shows ads. This was a firm design requirement: nothing interrupts you while you are actively tuning.
